🌱 Rootzy

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 22, 2026

The short version

  • Rootzy is used by parents to track their child's daily habits. Children do not create accounts or log in themselves.
  • We collect only what's needed to run the app: parent email, child info entered by the parent, habit data, optional milestone photos, and push notification tokens.
  • We never sell your data or your child's data, and we never use it for advertising.
  • You can review, edit, or delete your child's data at any time.

1. What Data We Collect

All information in Rootzy is entered by the parent through their own account. We collect:

DataWhy we collect it
Parent's email address To create and secure your account, and to sign you in
Child's name and profile info Entered by the parent, so the app can display and organize your child's habits
Habit tracking data Daily check-ins, streaks, and progress — the core function of the app
Milestone photos Optional photos a parent chooses to upload to celebrate their child's progress
Push notification token Lets us send reminders and milestone alerts to your device

We do not collect any information directly from children. See Section 4 for more detail.

2. How Data Is Stored

Rootzy's data is stored using Supabase, a managed backend provider. Habit data and account information live in a Postgres database, and milestone photos are stored using Supabase Storage. Data is protected using industry-standard encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and encryption at rest.

3. Data Sharing and Advertising

We want to be direct about this, because it matters most when children are involved:

  • We do not sell your data or your child's data to advertisers, data brokers, or anyone else, under any circumstances.
  • We do not use your child's data for targeted advertising or behavioral profiling.
  • We do not share your data or your child's data with third parties for marketing purposes.

Data is only shared with service providers that are strictly necessary to operate the app — for example, Supabase, which hosts our database, file storage, and authentication; and Expo/EAS's push notification infrastructure together with Apple's Push Notification service (APNs), which process device push tokens solely to deliver reminders and milestone alerts. These providers are contractually bound to protect your data and are not permitted to use it for their own purposes.

4. Children's Privacy (COPPA)

Rootzy is designed to be used by parents on behalf of their children. We built the app this way specifically to comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and to keep children's data safe:

  1. Accounts belong exclusively to parents. The Rootzy account is created and owned by the parent or guardian. Children do not register their own account, do not enter their own email address, and do not go through any account creation or sign-up flow themselves.
  2. Child information is entered by the parent, not collected from the child. Any information related to a child profile — name, other profile details, habit-tracking data, and photos — is voluntarily entered into the app by the parent on the child's behalf. We do not collect this information directly from the child at any point.
  3. Creating an account is how a parent gives consent. By creating a Rootzy account and agreeing to this Privacy Policy, the parent is providing consent for the collection and use of their child's information. Rootzy is designed for account creation and management by parents only.
  4. No stranger contact, no chat, no ads shown to children. Rootzy contains no features that allow children to communicate with strangers, no social or chat functionality of any kind, and no advertising is shown to children within the app.
  5. Parents can review, edit, or delete their child's data at any time. You can do this through in-app account deletion, or by contacting us directly (see Section 8). Before acting on any request to access or delete a child's data, we will verify that the request is coming from the parent who holds the account.
  6. Deletion is permanent. When an account is deleted, all child-related data — photos, habit records, and profile information — is permanently deleted within 30 days, except where we're required to retain certain records by law.

5. Data Retention

We retain your data, and your child's data, only for as long as your account remains active. If you close your account or request deletion, your data — including your child's information — is deleted as described in Section 4.

6. Security Measures

We take a number of steps to keep your data secure:

  • Encryption of data in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and at rest
  • Access controls that restrict who and what can read or modify your data
  • Reliance on Supabase's infrastructure security practices, including database-level row security policies scoped to each account

No system is 100% immune to risk, but we design Rootzy to minimize what data is collected and who can access it.

7. Changes to This Policy

If we update this Privacy Policy, we'll revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. For material changes — ones that meaningfully affect how we collect or use your or your child's data — we will also provide notice inside the app.
